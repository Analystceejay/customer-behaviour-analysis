# Customer-Behaviour-Analysis
Uncovering what drives purchasing decisions across 3,900 retail transactions.

## Tools Used
- Python (Pandas & NumPy — data wrangling and transformation)
- SQL
- Power BI

## Key Analysis Areas

- 1. Spending Pattern Analysis
Breakdown of average order values, high-spend vs low-spend segments, and category-level revenue distribution.

- 2. Customer Segmentation
Grouping customers by behaviour and profile to identify high-value, at-risk, and growth segments.

- 3. Product Preference Mapping
Understanding which categories drive the most volume and value, and identifying cross-purchase trends.

- 4. Subscription Behaviour
Analysing how subscription status correlates with purchase frequency, basket size, and customer lifetime value.

## Project Overview
This project dives into transactional retail data to answer a core business question: who are our customers, what do they buy, and why? Using a dataset of 3,900 purchases spanning multiple product categories, the analysis surfaces actionable insights into spending patterns, customer segmentation, product preferences, and subscription behaviour that shapes smarter marketing, inventory, and retention strategies.

## Objectives

- Identify distinct customer segments based on purchasing behaviour and demographics
- Analyse spending patterns across product categories and time periods
- Understand product preferences and cross-category purchasing trends
- Evaluate subscription behaviour and its relationship with purchase frequency and value
- Translate findings into strategic business recommendations
 
## Business Impact
The insights from this analysis directly support decisions around:

-  Targeted marketing and personalised promotions
-  Inventory planning and category investment
-  Subscription programme optimisation
-  Customer retention and churn reduction strategies
